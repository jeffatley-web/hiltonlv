Overview

A Las Vegas hotel property seeking innovative solutions to enhance the quality of water conditions in its swimming pools and spas — and cut water and chemical use in a desert climate.

In 2023, AquaRev Water engineers were introduced to the engineering and management teams at Hilton Grand Vacations Club Paradise in Las Vegas. The property — operating commercial pools and spas in the high-evaporation Mojave climate — had three priorities the existing chemical-treatment infrastructure couldn't fully address:

  • Reduce water loss and replacement-water demand in a desert market with rising water-scarcity pressure
  • Cut pool chemical consumption (liquid chlorine and muriatic acid) without compromising water quality
  • Improve guest-facing water clarity and reduce chemical odor at the pool and spa decks

After a technology review covering AquaRev's patents, laboratory data, and prior commercial-property performance, a structured 30-day pre / post pilot was approved on the property's Family Pool and Family Spa.

The Pilot

30-day measured pilot · two water bodies · independent third-party data capture.

The pilot was designed to produce defensible before/after results under real operating conditions, with daily measurement and chemical-use logging performed by an independent commercial pool service partner.

Pilot Assets
Family Pool + Family Spa — both representative property water bodies serving guests during the pilot.
Service Partner
Quality One Pool Management (Aquatic System Professionals) — captured daily pre- and post-install measurements using AquaRev's "HDC Pre and Post Data Collection" form.
Pilot Window
Pre-install: 14 measurement days in August 2023. Post-install: 14 measurement days from August 31 through September 19, 2023. Equivalent measurement-day windows enable apples-to-apples comparison; results are reported on a normalized 30-day basis.
Daily Measurements
Air temperature, weather, liquid-chlorine added (gal), muriatic acid added (gal), sodium bicarbonate added (lbs), water-meter reading, manual-fill amount, plus full chemistry: TCL, FCL, pH, ALK, calcium hardness, CYA.
Reported Results
Three measured reductions published as the headline AquaRev customer case study: 89% Water Loss · 41% Chlorine · 33% Acid — Family Pool basis.
Outcome
Validated reductions across both water bodies, with the Family Pool numbers landing on the published case study card.

Methodology & sourcing

How the numbers were measured.

  • Pilot window: 14 measurement days pre-install (Aug 11–30, 2023) and 14 measurement days post-install (Aug 31 – Sep 19, 2023). Family Pool and Family Spa measured in parallel using AquaRev's "HDC Pre and Post Data Collection" form. 30-day numbers extrapolated × 30/14.
  • Independent data capture: all daily measurements taken on-site by Quality One Pool Management (Aquatic System Professionals), the property's commercial pool service partner.
  • Daily metrics: air temperature, weather, liquid chlorine added (gal), muriatic acid added (gal), sodium bicarbonate added (lbs), water-meter reading, manual fill amount, and full chemistry panel — TCL, FCL, pH, ALK, calcium hardness, CYA.
  • Headline reductions: 89% water · 41% chlorine · 33% acid — calculated using the operator's weekly-average comparison method on Family Pool data, validated and published as the official AquaRev customer case study card. Sodium bicarbonate excluded due to a documented rain-driven anomaly in the post window.
  • Las Vegas utility rate: water $4.50/1,000 gal (LVVWD commercial water + Clark County sewer, est. 2025) — to be verified against current published schedule at deployment.
  • Chemical pricing: $4/gal liquid chlorine (12.5%) and $6/gal muriatic acid (31%) — mid-range commercial pool-supply pricing for the Las Vegas market, to be reconciled to property's invoiced unit prices.
  • Validation: three published reductions appear on AquaRev's official Hilton Grand Vacations Club Paradise customer case study card.
